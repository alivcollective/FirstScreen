## Hospital Partnership Model

### Partnership Tiers

#### Tier 1: Verified Partner (Free)
**Requirements:** Accreditation verification, basic info accuracy
**Benefits:**
- Verified badge on Health Compass listing
- Included in screening center search results
- Standard profile page

#### Tier 2: Enhanced Partner (฿30,000–80,000/year)
**Requirements:** Signed partnership agreement, data accuracy commitment
**Benefits:**
- Featured placement in provider search
- Detailed profile: photos, accreditations, specialties, insurance
- Appointment deep-link integration
- Patient education co-branding

#### Tier 3: Integration Partner (Revenue share)
**Requirements:** API integration, data feed, designated contact
**Benefits:**
- Real-time appointment booking within Health Compass
- Screening package listing and sales
- Health Compass-referred patient attribution
- Revenue share on attributed referrals
- Joint marketing campaigns

#### Tier 4: Strategic Partner (Custom)
**For:** Network-level partnerships (Bangkok Hospital Group, Samitivej Group)
**Benefits:**
- White-label patient app option
- Custom analytics dashboard
- Co-developed screening programs
- Joint PR and thought leadership

### What Hospitals Get

1. **High-intent patient acquisition** — Users who find hospitals through Health Compass have already been risk-stratified; they need the service
2. **Screening volume** — Structured screening campaigns drive predictable patient flow
3. **Patient education** — Better-prepared patients, shorter consultation time
4. **Quality signal** — Accreditation display on Thailand's most trusted health platform
5. **Population analytics** — Anonymized screening demand data for planning

### Hospital Data Requirements

Health Compass requires hospitals to provide and maintain:
- Organization details (name, address, accreditation, hours)
- Services offered with pricing ranges
- Insurance accepted
- Languages spoken by staff
- Booking contact / booking URL

Health Compass does NOT require or accept:
- Individual patient records
- Clinical outcome data
- Financial data

## Insurance Partnership Model

### The Prevention-Insurance Nexus

Health insurance companies spend vast sums treating diseases that could have been prevented. Health Compass offers insurers a direct channel to:
1. Attract health-conscious, prevention-motivated customers (better risk pool)
2. Engage policyholders with prevention programs (reduce claims)
3. Position as a health partner, not just a claims payer

### Partnership Products

#### Product 1: Marketplace Listing
- Insurance products displayed to risk-stratified users
- User has just completed diabetes risk assessment → AIA diabetes care plan presented
- Affiliate model: 15–25% of first-year premium

#### Product 2: Prevention-Linked Wellness Programs
- Insurer designs wellness program; Health Compass delivers the education content
- Policyholders earn insurance discounts for completing Health Compass health milestones
- Track screening completion, risk assessment participation

#### Product 3: Employer Group Health
- Insurer + Health Compass + Employer: tri-party wellness program
- Employer offers Health Compass Premium as employee benefit
- Insurer offers reduced group premium for participating employers
- Health Compass provides aggregate (anonymized) workforce health data

#### Product 4: Risk Score API
- Insurer API access to aggregate (never individual) population risk data
- Actuarial intelligence: screening rates, risk factor prevalence by geography and demographic band
- NOT individual user risk scores — aggregate only

### Insurance Data Rules

**What we share with insurance partners:**
- Aggregate, anonymized population-level insights (with user consent for analytics)
- Referred-user attribution (that a Health Compass user clicked through, not their health data)
- Program completion rates for wellness programs

**What we NEVER share:**
- Individual user health profiles
- Individual risk assessment results
- Individual symptom sessions
- Any data that could influence individual underwriting decisions

**Anti-discrimination commitment:** Health Compass will never provide data that enables insurance companies to discriminate against users based on their Health Compass activity.

## Government Partnership Model

### Why Governments Need Health Compass

**The Problem:**
National health campaigns historically reach low-awareness, low-motivation populations inefficiently. The people who need screening most are the hardest to reach.

**Health Compass Solution:**
- Meets users where they are (digital-first)
- Provides personalized, relevant information (not generic campaigns)
- Creates measurable action (appointments booked, screenings scheduled)
- Generates anonymized population health intelligence back to ministries

### Government Partnership Tiers

#### Tier 1: Content Partnership (Free/MOU)
- Align Health Compass content with national guidelines
- Ministry content reviewed and endorsed
- Ministry logo displayed as guideline source
- Ministry shares platform with citizens
- Examples: MOPH content endorsement, NHSO screening package alignment

#### Tier 2: Public Health Campaign Integration
- Ministry designs health campaign; Health Compass provides digital reach
- Joint co-branded campaigns (Cervical Cancer Month, Diabetes Awareness)
- Custom landing pages for national health programs
- Performance reporting: reach, engagement, screening facilitation

**Pricing:** Cost-sharing or co-investment model. Not purely commercial.

#### Tier 3: National Population Health Analytics
- Government receives aggregate anonymized health intelligence
- Screening rates by region, age, sex — measured in real-time
- Disease risk factor prevalence trends
- Healthcare access gap identification
- Pricing: ฿5M–50M/year depending on scope and population

#### Tier 4: National Integration
- Health Compass becomes part of national digital health infrastructure
- Integration with national health ID systems (privacy-preserving)
- Official screening reminder service for national programs
- API integration with national EMR/PHR systems
- Long-term government contracts

### Government Engagement Strategy

**Year 1 (2026):**
- MOPH: Guideline alignment MOU, content partnership
- NHSO: Preventive care package promotion partnership
- Health Promotion Foundation: Joint campaigns

**Year 2 (2027):**
- NHSO: Analytics contract — national screening rates dashboard
- MOPH: Joint national health campaign (diabetes, cervical cancer)
- Ministry of Digital Economy: Digital health platform recognition

**Year 3 (2028):**
- National digital health infrastructure role
- ASEAN health ministry network — regional data sharing framework

**WHO Partnership:**
- WHO SEARO: Regional guidelines source alignment
- WHO global: Partner platform for SDG health goals
- IHR (International Health Regulations): Disease surveillance integration

## Research Partnership Model

### Academic & Research Institution Partnerships

**University Partners (Thailand):**
- Mahidol University — Research data, faculty review board
- Chulalongkorn University — Clinical studies
- Khon Kaen University — Rural health research

**Partnership Types:**

1. **Medical Advisory Board** — Faculty provide content review; credited; modest honorarium
2. **Research Data Access** — IRB-approved aggregate data for public health research (free)
3. **Collaborative Research** — Joint studies using Health Compass as recruitment/education platform
4. **Student Health Education** — Medical/nursing students create content under faculty supervision

**Research Ethics Framework:**
- IRB approval required for all research data requests
- Data always aggregate, never individual-level
- User consent required for any research participation
- Published findings shared back to Health Compass community
